# HouseCheck — 5-Minute Pitch

Slide-by-slide outline + talk track. ~40 seconds per slide. Lead with the demo energy; end with the ask.

---

### 1 · Title (0:20)
**Slide:** "HouseCheck — Carfax for apartments." Live URL. The 24-vs-78 gauges.
**Say:** Hook them with the two-building spread. *"Two real Brooklyn buildings, blocks apart: 24 and 78. By the end of this you'll know how we tell them apart in seconds — on real data, live right now."*

### 2 · Problem (0:40)
**Slide:** 3 numbers — 51.6% rent-burdened · $1,761 stay-vs-move gap · 11.1M violation records.
**Say:** A $40k blind decision. The data exists but is unusable, scattered across three government portals. New laws (FARE Act, Good Cause) make it worse — renters have rights they can't exercise without data.

### 3 · Solution (0:40)
**Slide:** the Building Health Card — 4 axes.
**Say:** Type an address → one 0–100 score: condition, legal protections, rent fairness, accessibility. Every number linked to a government source. The edge is the *trust model*, not any single feature.

### 4 · Demo (0:50) — **the centerpiece**
**Slide:** live, or the recorded demo clip.
**Say:** Hit the live URL. 61 Stuyvesant → 24, 65 open violations. 510 Quincy → 192 stabilized units from tax records. *"This is deployed, public, serving 250 real buildings."*

### 5 · Why we're different (0:40)
**Slide:** "Government data only. Every number sourced. We refuse to fake it."
**Say:** Competitors use crowdsourced reviews (OpenIgloo) or unusable gov portals. We fact-checked our own pitch and cut fabricated stats. Where data can't prove stabilization, we say "unverified." Honesty is the product.

### 6 · Results (0:40)
**Slide:** stat row — live URL · 250 buildings · 87 stabilized · 76 tests green · $0 data cost.
**Say:** Built in ~10 days. Rust backend, real data pipeline, green CI on three OSes, deployed for ~$0. It works, today.

### 7 · Business (0:35)
**Slide:** free consumer → freemium → B2B SaaS; $3.6B adjacent market.
**Say:** Renters are cash-strapped, so we go B2B2C — free tool builds trust and users; landlords/insurers pay for the data. Credit-Karma-for-apartments.

### 8 · The ask / close (0:35)
**Slide:** live URL + repo + "the team."
**Say:** *"HouseCheck turns a blind $40,000 decision into an informed one — on free, honest, public data. It's live at housecheck-nessa.fly.dev. Thank you."*

---

**Delivery notes:** rehearse to 4:30 so you have buffer. Don't read slides — they're backdrop. Spend your energy on slides 1 and 4. Have the live URL loaded and a backup screen-recording in case wifi dies. Know your two "wow" facts cold: **24 vs 78**, and **192 stabilized units from tax records**.
